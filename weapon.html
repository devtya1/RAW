Service Weapon

Beretta M9

The Beretta M9 is a 9mm semi-automatic pistol that has been the standard sidearm for the United States Armed Forces since 1985. It is known for its reliability, accuracy, and durability. The M9 is capable of holding a 15-round magazine, and its design includes features such as an ambidextrous safety, combat trigger guard, and a reversible magazine release.
